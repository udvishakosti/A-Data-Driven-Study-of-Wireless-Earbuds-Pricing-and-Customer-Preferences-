Business Problem

Rohit is a working professional who wants to buy a new pair of wireless earbuds. He visits multiple online shopping platforms and finds hundreds of earbuds models with differences in:

Prices

Bluetooth versions

Discount percentages

Brand variations

Available colors

Customer ratings

Some earbuds cost 800, while others go up to 8,000 or more, yet many of them appear to offer similar features. This creates confusion for Rohit while making a purchase decision.

Rohit starts asking questions:

Is paying a higher price really worth it?

Do higher discounts actually mean better value?

Do premium brands always have better customer ratings?

Does a higher Bluetooth version significantly increase the price?

Which combination of features gives the best value for money?

Like Rohit, many online buyers face similar confusion while purchasing wireless earbuds due to pricing complexity and feature overlap.

Business Problem Statement

In todays e-commerce market, customers have access to a wide variety of wireless earbuds from multiple brands, Bluetooth versions, and price ranges. While this variety provides more choices, it also increases confusion, as many earbuds appear similar in specifications but differ significantly in MRP, selling price, discounts, and customer ratings.

This project aims to address this problem by performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on wireless earbuds data containing information such as Brand, Bluetooth Version, Color, MRP, Selling Price, Discount Percentage, Discount Amount, and Customer Ratings. The analysis focuses on understanding how pricing, discounts, and technology influence customer perception and overall product value.

The study also evaluates whether:

Higher-priced earbuds receive better ratings

Newer Bluetooth versions command higher prices

Higher discounts truly offer better value for money

By identifying key patterns and relationships within the data, this project helps in:

Understanding how selling price varies with brand and Bluetooth version

Identifying which factors contribute most to higher pricing

Analyzing the relationship between discounts and customer ratings

Highlighting earbuds that offer the best value for money

Ultimately, the objective of this analysis is to help customers make smarter, data-driven purchasing decisions and assist businesses in optimizing their pricing and discount strategies in the competitive wireless earbuds market.

Hypothesis Test 1: Does Discount affect Selling Price?

Does a higher discount percentage significantly reduce the selling price?

Hypotheses

H (Null): There is no significant difference in selling price between low and high discount products

H (Alternative): Products with higher discounts have significantly lower selling prices

low_disc = df[df["Discount_Level"] == "Low"]["Selling_Price"]
high_disc = df[df["Discount_Level"] == "High"]["Selling_Price"]

t_stat, p_value = stats.ttest_ind(low_disc, high_disc)

print("p-value:", p_value)

p-value: 1.7058956857955855e-19

Conclusion

If p-value < 0.05 Reject H

Conclusion: Higher discounts significantly reduce selling prices

Final Conclusion

  1. Data Collection Conclusion

Wireless earbuds data was collected focusing on key attributes such as Brand, Bluetooth Version, Color, MRP, Selling Price, Discount Percentage, Discount Amount, and Customer Ratings. The dataset successfully represents a wide range of products across different price segments and brands, making it suitable for analyzing market trends and customer preferences.

  1. Data Cleaning Conclusion

Data cleaning techniques were applied to improve data quality by handling missing values, removing duplicates, correcting inconsistent values, and managing extreme outliers. Numerical columns were standardized, logical constraints were enforced (such as Selling Price MRP), and outliers were controlled to ensure reliable and meaningful analysis. This step ensured that the dataset was accurate and ready for exploratory analysis.

  1. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Conclusion

EDA revealed that the wireless earbuds market is dominated by a few popular brands, with most products falling into the budget and mid-range price categories. Newer Bluetooth versions (5.2 and 5.3) were more common, indicating increasing adoption of advanced technology. Customer ratings were generally high, though some low-rating outliers highlighted quality variations. Discount patterns showed aggressive pricing strategies across certain brands.

  1. Feature Engineering Conclusion

Custom features such as Discount Amount and Value-for-Money indicators enhanced the dataset by providing deeper insights into pricing efficiency and customer perception. These engineered features helped compare products beyond basic pricing and allowed better evaluation of whether discounts and prices justified customer ratings.

  1. Outlier Analysis Conclusion

Outliers in MRP, Selling Price, Discount Percentage, and Ratings were identified and analyzed. These outliers represented either premium-priced products or unusually high discounts and low ratings. Handling these outliers improved statistical stability while also demonstrating real-world pricing irregularities present in e-commerce platforms.

  1. Hypothesis Testing Conclusion

Statistical hypothesis testing confirmed that discount levels significantly impact selling prices, brand category influences customer ratings, and Bluetooth version has a measurable effect on pricing. These results validated assumptions made during EDA and provided statistical evidence for observed trends, strengthening the analytical conclusions.

  1. Business Insights Conclusion

The analysis showed that higher prices do not always guarantee higher customer ratings. Customers tend to value a balanced combination of price, discount, technology, and brand reputation rather than cost alone. Products offering moderate prices with higher discounts and newer Bluetooth versions often deliver better perceived value.
